Sikkim-Travel Guide: Best Place to Visit


Highest Lakes in India Live in Sikkim | June 30,2024

Sikkim, a small yet enchanting state nestled in the northeastern region of India, is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, rich biodiversity, and vibrant culture. Surrounded by the majestic Himalayas, Sikkim is home to the third-highest peak in the world, Kanchenjunga, which serves as a backdrop to its serene valleys and lush forests. The state's geography is characterized by steep mountains, cascading rivers, and extensive agricultural lands, primarily dedicated to organic farming, including the famous Sikkimese cardamom. Sikkim is also distinguished by its diverse flora and fauna, with numerous protected areas and wildlife sanctuaries. The state's rich tapestry of culture is a result of various ethnic communities, including the Lepchas, Bhutias, and Nepalis, which contribute to a vibrant mix of traditions, languages, and cuisines, often highlighting local staples like rice, maize, and the delicious momos. Visitors can immerse themselves in Sikkim's heritage through its colorful festivals, monasteries, and markets, making the experience both enriching and unforgettable.The best time to visit Sikkim largely depends on the type of experience travelers seek. Generally, the ideal time is between March and June and then again from September to November. During spring (March to May), Sikkim comes alive with blooming rhododendrons and vibrant wildflowers, making it perfect for nature enthusiasts and photographers. The weather during these months is pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 25°C (50°F to 77°F), ideal for trekking and exploring its natural beauty. The monsoon season from June to August can bring heavy rainfall, making many trekking routes difficult and leading to landslides, so it’s best to avoid this period if possible. However, the post-monsoon months of September to November also offer clear skies and stunning views, making it a favorite time for tourists aiming to witness the snow-capped peaks against a brilliant blue sky.

• Main Places to Visit

1. Tsomgo Lake:

Tsomgo Lake, also known as Changu Lake, is a stunning high-altitude glacial lake located in East Sikkim, India, at an elevation of approximately 3,750 meters (12,303 feet). Renowned for its breathtaking beauty, the lake is crescent-shaped and covers around 1 kilometer (0.6 miles), with its water levels fluctuating with the seasons, being cold and pristine due to its glacial origins. Surrounded by snow-capped mountains, the lake provides picturesque views, especially during spring when vibrant rhododendrons bloom. Tsomgo Lake holds significant cultural and religious importance for the local population, being considered sacred and tied to various myths, particularly those associated with Guru Padmasambhava. Accessible by road from Gangtok, about 40 kilometers (25 miles) away, the lake attracts many tourists, who can enjoy pony or yak rides in the surrounding areas. The stunning landscape offers excellent photography opportunities, particularly as it transforms dramatically across the seasons, with winter snowfall creating a serene white vista and summer revealing its striking blue waters. Due to its proximity to the Indo-China border, visitors need special permits to access the lake, adding an element of exclusivity to this natural wonder. Additionally, the area is rich in biodiversity, providing a habitat for migratory birds and unique flora, making it a vital ecological zone in the Himalayas.


2. Nathula Pass:

Nathula Pass, located in East Sikkim at an altitude of 4,310 meters (14,100 feet), is a significant mountain pass that links India and Tibet, making it a vital part of the ancient Silk Route. This strategically important pass is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes and strategic military significance, being a crucial point for trade and cultural exchange between India and Tibet. The pass is accessible from Gangtok, approximately 54 kilometers (34 miles) away, and it requires special permits for visitors, highlighting its restricted and sensitive nature. Nathula experiences extreme weather conditions, with heavy snowfall during the winter months, typically from October to March, which adds to its allure as a winter wonderland. The pass is also famous for its stunning panoramic views of the Eastern Himalayas, showcasing snow-capped peaks and lush valleys, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Tourists visiting Nathula can explore the beautiful landscape while also engaging with the rich cultural interactions that occur at the Indo-China border. Visitors can witness the changing of the guard ceremony at the Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP) post, which echoes the historical and ongoing military significance of the area.In addition to its natural beauty, Nathula is also a hub for local flora and fauna, with diverse species that thrive in these high-altitude conditions. The vibrant Rhododendron blooms in spring add to the pass's charm. Moreover, the area is dotted with small souvenir shops where local artisans sell traditional items. The allure of Nathula Pass lies not only in its breathtaking scenery and cultural richness but also in its historical significance as a once-bustling trade route, making it a unique destination in Sikkim.


3. Gurudongmar Lake:

Gurudongmar Lake, situated at an impressive altitude of 5,430 meters (17,819 feet) in North Sikkim, is one of the highest lakes in the world and a stunning natural wonder known for its breathtaking beauty and spiritual significance. Surrounded by snow-capped peaks and stark landscapes, the lake is revered by both Buddhists and Sikhs, who believe it to be holy. Legend has it that Guru Padmasambhava, a revered figure in Tibetan Buddhism, blessed the lake, which is reflected in its serene waters. The lake's unique turquoise color contrasts spectacularly with the rugged mountains, creating a picturesque setting that draws adventurers and nature lovers alike. Access to Gurudongmar Lake involves a journey of about 190 kilometers (118 miles) from Gangtok, with permits required for visitors due to its proximity to the Indo-Tibet border. The journey takes travelers through winding roads, revealing the stark beauty of the Himalayas and offering glimpses of local wildlife, including the elusive snow leopard. Despite its remote location, the lake is known for its ethereal landscapes, which change with the seasons; in the summer, it thaws into a vibrant blue expanse, while winter transforms it into a stunning icy vista. The area around Gurudongmar Lake is also home to diverse flora and fauna, with several endemic species thriving in the harsh mountain environment. Visitors can engage in serene activities like meditation or simply enjoy the tranquil ambiance, making it a favored destination for spiritual seekers. The combination of altitude, natural beauty, and cultural significance makes Gurudongmar Lake a unique gem in Sikkim, beckoning those in search of solitude and breathtaking vistas amidst the majestic Himalayas.


4. MG Marg:

MG Marg, officially known as Mahatma Gandhi Marg, is the vibrant heart of Gangtok, the capital city of Sikkim. This pedestrian-only thoroughfare is renowned for its cleanliness, beautiful surroundings, and lively atmosphere, making it a popular destination for both locals and tourists. Spanning approximately 1.2 kilometers (0.75 miles), MG Marg serves as a central hub where visitors can stroll through well-paved walkways lined with colorful flowers, contemporary shops, and eateries offering local and international cuisines. The area is particularly famous for its vibrant marketplaces where travelers can purchase traditional Sikkimese handicrafts, woolen garments, and unique souvenirs, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the region. Local street vendors and established shops contribute to a lively shopping experience, while various food stalls provide mouthwatering delicacies such as momos (dumplings) and thukpa (noodle soup). MG Marg is also a cultural hotspot, frequently hosting events, music performances, and festivals that reflect the rich traditions of Sikkim, creating an inviting atmosphere that draws visitors to engage with the local culture. At night, the street comes alive with beautiful lighting, enhancing the charm of the area as people gather to enjoy the ambiance. Moreover, MG Marg is conveniently located near major attractions, including the Enchey Monastery and the Gangtok Ropeway, making it an ideal starting point for exploring the city. The road's significance extends beyond commerce and tourism; it embodies the essence of Gangtok's modern urban life while embracing its rich heritage. With its lively vibe, diverse offerings, and picturesque views of the surrounding hills, MG Marg stands out as a must-visit location in Sikkim, appealing to all who wish to experience the local culture and hospitality.
