1. OOTY:

Ooty, also known as Udagamandalam, is a beautiful hill station located in the Nilgiri district of Tamil Nadu. It is situated at an altitude of 2,240 meters above sea level and is known for its stunning views, cool climate, and picturesque surroundings. Ooty is a popular vacation spot for tourists from all over India and is known for its breathtaking views, colonial-style architecture, and delicious cuisine. One of the top attractions in Ooty is the Ooty Lake, which is a man-made lake that was built in 1824. It is a popular spot for boating and picnics, and visitors can also enjoy a stroll along the lake's banks and take in the stunning views of the surrounding hills.

2. MANGALORE:

Mangaluru, located on the shores of the Arabian Sea in the southwestern state of Karnataka, is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage, stunning beaches, and delectable cuisine. Known for its historic temples, bustling markets, and picturesque landscapes, Mangaluru is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by travellers. Mangaluru boasts a diverse cultural heritage, influenced by centuries of history and trade. The city is home to numerous temples, including the famous Kadri Manjunath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, and the Kudroli Gokarnath Temple, built by the revered social reformer Sri Narayana Guru. Visitors can also explore the historic churches and mosques that reflect the city's multicultural fabric.

3. SIKKIM:

Sikkim, a small yet enchanting state nestled in the northeastern region of India, is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, rich biodiversity, and vibrant culture. Surrounded by the majestic Himalayas, Sikkim is home to the third-highest peak in the world, Kanchenjunga, which serves as a backdrop to its serene valleys and lush forests. The state's geography is characterized by steep mountains, cascading rivers, and extensive agricultural lands, primarily dedicated to organic farming, including the famous Sikkimese cardamom. Sikkim is also distinguished by its diverse flora and fauna, with numerous protected areas and wildlife sanctuaries.

4. Nainital:

Nainital, often referred to as the "Lake District of India," is a picturesque hill station nestled in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, situated at an elevation of about 2,084 meters (6,837 feet) above sea level. The town is built around a serene crescent-shaped lake, Naini Lake, which is dotted with vibrant pedal boats and surrounded by lush green hills, making it a favorite destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Nainital's lush landscapes, crystal-clear waters, and cool climate offer a refreshing escape from the sweltering heat of the plains, attracting tourists year-round. The mesmerizing Naini Lake is the centerpiece of the town and is surrounded by a charming promenade known as Mall Road. This bustling pathway is lined with shops, restaurants, and cafes, providing visitors with various options for shopping and dining. Popular activities at Naini Lake include boating, yachting, and taking leisurely walks along the lakeside. The lake is also steeped in mythology, believed to be the eye of the goddess Sati, and holds immense religious significance; hence, the town attracts pilgrims throughout the year.

7. PORT BLAIR:

Port Blair is the capital of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, and the largest city in the archipelago. Visitors are often overwhelmed by the sheer variety of options of activities and places to visit in Port Blair. We have made a list of a few places you absolutely must visit on your next trip to this idyllic tropical paradise.Once a prison under the British regime, this complex was declared a national memorial by Morarji Desai, the Indian Prime Minister in 1979. It held many freedom fighters captive whom the British considered dangerous on the mainland. Today, it houses a museum and hospital, in stark contrast to the horrors that it was once privy to.

A road trip to Ladakh is no small feat- it is well and truly the stuff of dreams. Ladakh’s soundlessness, its cobalt blue skies, bright rainbows, and glistening lakes are pure magic.
